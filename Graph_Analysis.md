This line chart analyzes the financial performance of the healthcare system
over multiple years by comparing **Total Revenue** and **Total Expenses**.

### Data Bifurcation:
- X-axis: Year
- Y-axis: Financial Value
- Measures compared: Total Revenue and Expense

### Detailed Analysis:
- The chart shows how revenue and expenses evolve year by year.
- A steady increase in revenue indicates growing healthcare demand and service expansion.
- Rising expenses reflect higher operational costs such as staffing, infrastructure,
  treatment costs, and medical supplies.
- The gap between revenue and expenses represents profitability.
- Years where expenses grow faster than revenue indicate potential cost inefficiencies.

### Business Importance:
This analysis helps management evaluate financial sustainability, plan budgets,
and identify periods where cost control measures are required.
